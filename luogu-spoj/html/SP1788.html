FRACTAN - Fractan

题目描述

To play the "fraction game" corresponding to a given list `f $ _{1} $ , f $ _{2} $ , ..., f $ _{k} $ ` of fractions and starting integer `N`, you repeatedly multiply the integer you have at any stage (initially `N`) by the earliest `f $ _{i} $ ` in the list for which the answer is integral. Whenever there is no such `f $ _{i} $ `, the game stops. Formally, we define a sequence by `S $ _{0} $ =N`, and `S $ _{j+1} $ =f $ _{i} $ S $ _{j} $ `, if for `1<=i<=k`, the number `f $ _{i} $ S $ _{j} $ ` is an integer but the numbers `f $ _{1} $ S $ _{j} $ , ..., f $ _{i-1} $ S $ _{j} $ ` are not. For example, if we have the list of eight fractions `f $ _{1} $ =170/39`, `f $ _{2} $ =19/13`, `f $ _{3} $ =13/17`, `f $ _{4} $ =69/95`, `f $ _{5} $ =19/23`, `f $ _{6} $ =1/19`, `f $ _{7} $ =13/7`, `f $ _{8} $ =1/3`, and start with `N=21`, we produce the (finite) sequence `(21,39,170,130,190,138,114,6,2)`. In general, the sequence may be infinite. Given a fraction list and a starting integer calculate a part of the defined sequence. Actually, we are interested only in the powers of `2` that appear in the sequence.
