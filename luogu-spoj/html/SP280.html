LIFTS - Lifts

题意翻译

``` Description Serj非常喜欢爱玩老游戏，最近他迷上了一种游戏，他需要控制着游戏的主人公在地图上寻找搜集各种情报，可就在游戏过程中，在主人公的面前出现了一个大坑，主人公必须走过大坑才能继续游戏。 在大坑里有着一个个紧挨着的电梯，其实看起来就是一根根会自动竖直升降的木桩。可木桩在不断地上升下降，主人公只有在两根木桩达到同一水平面的时候才能从一根的边缘走上另一根。每根木桩都宽4米，开始的时候主人公距离大坑有2米的距离，他一启动，所有的木桩都按照限定的方向开始升降，他首先要走过这2米的距离才到达大坑的边缘。而主人公每秒钟能移动2米的距离，因此，如果某一时刻主人公恰好在一根木桩的中央，他需要非常准确地启动，在1秒钟后到达该木桩的一端，如果这时候恰好另一根相邻的木桩升降到同一水平线的话，主人公就可以恰好走上去。 大坑的两头又恰好在同一水平线上，对于每根木桩，其初始的位置和开始时竖直移动的方向，以及其竖直起降的范围都已知了。无论上下，每根木桩移动的速度都是每秒钟1米。 请您编写一个程序，判断一下是否主人公能通过这一大坑，从而继续游戏。如果能通过，请找出走过这一大坑,再向前走2米后，所需要的最短时间。 Input 第一行为整数t，代表数据总数 每组数据的第一行将有一个整数N,表示大坑中电梯（木桩）的数量，N≤100。接下来的第i行每行将有4个整数，来说明依次点过去第i个电梯（木桩）的有关情况，分别记作Li,Ui,Si,Di。(-100 <= li <= si <= ui <= 100, l1 < ui) - Li表示该木桩移动到的最低点的水平高度 - Ui表示该木桩移动到的最高点的水平高度 - Si表示该木桩初始移动时的水平高度 - Di表示该木桩初始时的移动方向，1 表示上升，-1 表示下降 Output 应该是t行 每行代表每组数据的解。即为主人公移动的最短时间，如果主人公无法通过大坑，请输出-1。 由 @Venus 提供翻译

题目描述

Serj likes old games very much. Recently he has found one arcade game in his computer. When controlling the hero it is necessary to move on a map and collect various items. At a certain stage of the game Serj has faced an unexpected problem. To continue his adventures the hero should get past over a chasm. For this purpose it is possible to use consistently located lifts which look like horizontal platforms. Each lift moves up-down vertically between some levels. The hero can pass between the next adjacent platform, however it can be done only at the moment when they are at the same level. Similarly, passing from the edge of a chasm onto the lift and vice versa is only possible at the moment when the lift appears on the level of the edge. Each lift has a width equal to 4 meters. At the beginning the hero is in at a distance of two meters from the edge of a chasm. He should finish travel two meters after the opposite edge of the chasm. The hero moves at a speed of 2 meters a second. Thus, if the hero is in the initial position or in the center of the lift and wishes to pass to the next lift (or to descend from last lift onto the opposite edge of a chasm), he should begin movement exactly one second before they meet at one level. In two seconds the hero appears in the center of the next lift (or in the final position on the other side). The edges of the chasm are at the same level. For each lift the range of heights between which it moves, its initial position and the direction of movement at the initial moment are given. All lifts move with a speed of one meter a second. Find out whether the hero can get over to the opposite edge of the chasm, and if so what the minimal time required for this purpose is. ![A sample illustration](https://cdn.luogu.com.cn/upload/vjudge_pic/SP280/9c42f14fc6aecda21438759b42e220a8d6189e75.png)

输入输出格式

输入格式


_t_ – the number of test cases, then _t_ test cases follows. \[empty line\] A test case begins with _n_ - the number of lifts, a positive integer (_n_ <= 100), then n lines follow. The i-th line (_0 < i <= n_) contains four integers _li ui si di_, where: _li_ - lowest position of the lift, _ui_ - highest position of the lift, _si_ - initial position of the lift, _di_ - initial direction of movement (1 means up, -1 means down); (-100 <= _li_ <= _si_ <= _ui_ <= 100, _l1_ < _ui_).

输出格式


For each test case output the minmal time in seconds, required to get to the opposite edge of the chasm. If it is impossible output -1.
