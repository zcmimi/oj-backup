TELECOM - Telecommunications

题目描述

From beacons over telegraphs and telephones towards radio and television and ultimately computer networks and internet – telecommunication systems knew a tremendous evolution within the last century! This was also clear to Richard Hamming, who was bothered by erroneous data transmission. As the transmission channel is often very noisy, the received information is not necessarily the information that was sent out. Looking at digital data transmission, a straight-forward way to check for errors would be a supplementary bit of information, a so-called parity bit. Each package of data on _n-1_ bits is completed by 1 parity bit, whose value is chosen such that the number of ones in the _n_-bit package is even. Although there is little overhead, parity checking is not very robust. If an even number of bits is flipped during transmission, the check bit remains valid and the error will not be detected. Moreover, although parity can detect errors, it provides no indication on the position of the flipped bit.
